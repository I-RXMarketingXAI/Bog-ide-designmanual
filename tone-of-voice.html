02 Tone of Voice

Passionate. Knowing. Inviting.

The brand should sound like a culturally engaged guide: informed, clear, warm, and occasionally surprising, never institutional or over-explained.

Use This Voice
  • Write with warmth and confidence.
  • Prefer perspective over product-description language.
  • Make messages easy to feel, not just easy to decode.

Tone Pillars

Passionate

We genuinely care about books, creativity, and what they unlock.

Knowing

We share perspective without sounding superior or academic.

Inspirational

We open doors instead of listing features.

Good Company

We should feel like a welcome presence, not a script.

Do / Don't

Do

  • Lead with a human angle.
  • Use concrete, lively language.
  • Write like someone with taste and enthusiasm.

Don't

  • Sound bureaucratic or educational-by-default.
  • Use empty campaign slogans.
  • List products with no editorial point of view.

By Channel

Retail & POS

Short, immediate, and atmospheric. Catch attention fast and let the format do one job well.

Organic Social

Make it conversational, opinionated, and participatory. Avoid lifeless product grids.

Paid Media

Sharper and more direct, but still rooted in a situation or felt need.

Email

The most personal channel. Write as if a real bookseller is sending it.
